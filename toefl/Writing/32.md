I see the idea of requiring a study skills course, but I don't think it's a good one. A mandatory class for everyone is not efficient. It forces students who already have good study habits to sit through lessons they don't need. This can make them disengaged and uninterested. Also, this requirement doesn't address the real issue. The main problem is that students are not learning these skills in high school. That is the better place to teach good study habits. In university, support should be available as an option, like optional workshops. This is better than making all students take a class.

I do not think teachers should have the same salary as surgeons. There is no doubt that teachers play a vital role in our society. However, I believe pay should reflect several key factors. These include the amount of specialized training required, the level of responsibility, and the economic impact of the job. Surgeons spend more than a decade in very difficult and specific education. Every day, they make decisions that can change or even save a patient's life. Therefore, a more practical goal is to make sure teachers receive a truly fair and professional wage. This wage should recognize their important contribution without making an unrealistic comparison to a field with very different demands and risks.

I also consider making university education free to be a misguided policy. The goal of increasing access to education is understandable. However, I believe this policy would create more problems than it solves. A major issue is that it could devalue a university degree. If everyone can attend for free, the number of graduates would rise dramatically, potentially making a degree less valuable to employers. Furthermore, the enormous financial cost would not disappear; it would simply be transferred from students to taxpayers. This could place a heavy and unsustainable burden on society. A better solution is to improve targeted financial aid for students who need it most, which ensures access without compromising the system's quality or financial health.
