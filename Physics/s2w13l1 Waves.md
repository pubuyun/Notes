$$x = A\sin(\omega(t-t_{0}))+x_{0}$$
where:
$A$ is *amplitude*
$\omega=\frac{2\pi}{T}$, $T$ is *time period*, the time taken for 1 complete oscillation, measured in $s$
$f=\frac{1}{T}$, where $f$ is *frequency*, measured in $Hz$ or $s^{-1}$
$\phi=\omega\Delta t$, where $\phi$ is *phase*, an angle-like quantity representing the fraction of the cycle covered up to $t$.
$\lambda$ is *wave length*
	$v=f\lambda$, where $v$ is *phase velocity*, any given phase of the wave (for example, the **peak**) will appear to travel at the phase velocity

$$\frac{\sin\theta_{1}}{\sin \theta_{2}} = \frac{n_{2}}{n_{1}} = \frac{v_{1}}{v_{2}}$$

where:
$v_{1}, v_{2}$ is the *phase velocity*
$n_{1}, n_{2} = \frac{c}{v_{1}}, \frac{c}{v_{2}}$, is the *refractive index*
