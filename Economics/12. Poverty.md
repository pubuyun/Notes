> [!def]
> Poverty occurs when individuals or households are *deprived* of **material possessions** and **income** which stops them from having a *decent standard of living*

> **Absolute Poverty**: individuals or households are deprived of basic human needs(food, shelter, energy...)
> **Relative Poverty**: individuals or households are poor in comparison to the rest of the population
> **Inequalities** in the *distribution of income* and wealth are a main reason of poverty.

> **International Poverty line**: a global measurement of poverty, by comparing purchasing power of individuals within a country in dollar terms, PPP(purchasing power parity) used.
> **Poverty Gap Index** measures how far individuals are from the poverty line
## Causes
### Low wages and unemployment
- Average earnings growth has outstripped that of unemployment benefit payments
- Low skilled workers have found earnings are not enough to pay for basics such as heating
### Age and illness
- Many retired people rely on the state pension
- linked to inflation but had fallen behind the level of real earnings
- older people are more likely to suffer relative poverty
- In some, being off ill means no income
### Distribution of income
- more people live below the poverty line
- less fair and less inclusive society
	- Increased crime
	- A less safe env for all
	- Social tension
## Policies
### Promoting economic growth
- Economic growth will lead to benefits for society as a whole
- Incomes will increase and there will be greater demand for workers, so employment will go up
### Improved education
- An educated workforce will have more opportunities to obtain work
- As human capital increases, the individual is worth more to the firm
- Educated individuals are more likely to have the ability to set up their own businesses
### More generous state benefits
### Progressive taxation
- A progressive tax will increase the proportion of tax as workers earn more, a regressive is the opposite
### National minimum wage
- Higher incomes for low paid workers
- less absolute and relative poverty
