> [!def] 
> an association which represents the interests of a group of workers.
### Function
- Fight and bring to an end child labour in many countries
- Improve workers' safety
- Increase wages for both union and non-unionized workers
- Improve education and other benefits for many poor and working class families.
## Industrial actions
> [!def]
> when workers disrupt production to put pressure on employers to agree to their demands.

**Overtime ban**: workers refuse to work more than their normal hours
**Work to rule**: workers deliberately slow down production by complying rigidly with every rule and regulation
**Go-slow**: Workers carry out tasks deliberate.
***Strike***: a group of workers stopping work to put pressure on an employer to agree to their demands.
## Benefits
- Advice if dismissed (unfairly or made redundant)
- Improved communications between employees and management
- Improved pay
- Advice if unfairly treated
- Improved conditions of employment
- Employment if there is a closed shop
- Possible influence on government decisions
- Services such as insurance, social facilities
- Improved working conditions
- Strength in numbers
- Representation in grievances with management
## Aim
- negotiating improvements in wages and other non-wage benefits
- defending **employee's** rights and jobs
- improving **working conditions**
## Disadvantages
- trade unions can organise strikes if they do not receive the pay levels and work conditions they demand
- wages are likely to be higher - adding to business costs - when many employees are trade union members
- Extract benefits from businesses and taxpayers and other members as well so there is paid subscription, on some cases, limited transparency on how the fund are used
## Types
**Craft Unions**: Union of Operators and Technicians in Cinema
**Non-manual unions**: e.g. German Police Union
**Industrial unions**: Worker's Union
