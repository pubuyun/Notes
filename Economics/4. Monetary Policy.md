> [!def]
> The manipulation of the rate of interest, the money supply and exchange rates to influence the level of economic activity

**Target**: Low Inflation
### Expansionary Monetary Policy
> Money Supply **Higher**
> Interest Rate **Lower**
> Promote economic activity, AD shift right
### Contractionary Monetary Policy
> Money Supply **Lower**
> Interest Rate **Higher**
> Limit economic activity, AD shift left
## Money Supply
> The total amount of money in the economy at a particular time.
### Narrow Money
> The basic amount of notes and coins and operational deposits/reserve balances at Central Bank. 
> Approximately equal to the amount of cash in circulation
### Broad Money
> All notes and coins and deposits in saving accounts and other less liquid assets.
> Most inclusive definition of the money supply.
### Liquidity
> [!def]
> The ease with which can asset can be changed into cash.
### Controls
#### Increase
- Print more money
- Buy back government bonds
#### Decrease
- sell government bonds
- increase reserve requirement
## Interest Rate
Decrease in IR
less attractive to foreign investor
money flowing out
weaker currency (less value comparing to other)
higher export (other countries got cheaper price)
pay more for import (potential inflation)
## Foreign Rates
buying their own money on foreign currency exchange market
value of that money increases
central bank keep foreign currency reserves to do that
for firms in their country cheaper to buy other country's products
## Central Bank
**Maintain financial stability in the monetary system**
> lender of last resort

**Help government maintain macroeconomics stability**
> maintain price stability by setting low interest rates to lower inflation
