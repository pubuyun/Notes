## Government Budget
> [!def]
> A **financial plan** that *forecast* the government both revenue and spending over **a period of time**, usually a fiscal year.

*Budget deficit*: Public Expenditure > Tax revenue
*Budget surplus*: Public expenditure < Tax revenue
### Revenue
#### Tax revenues
- Income tax
- corporation tax
- Inheritance tax
- sales tax
- import taxes
#### Non-tax revenues
- Fines, tolls, fees and penalties
- Foreign Aid
- Loans from banks
- revenue from SOE's
- Donations
#### Taxation
> high taxes can fund essential services
> but may reduce disposable income and investment.
#### Borrowing
> can finance immediate needs,
> excessive debt may lead to higher interest payments and long-term fiscal challenges
#### Grants and Aid
> support specific projects or initiatives 
> but may come with conditions that influence domestic policy
#### Monetary Financing
> can stimulate the economy 
> but risks inflation if not managed carefully
#### Asset Sales
> provide immediate revenue
> it may reduce long term income from those assets

## Taxation
### Reasons
- **To redistribute income from from the rich to the poor**: Higher income earners pay more tax.
- **To discourage the consumption of demerit goods**: cigarettes & alcohol.
- **To raise the costs of firms that has negative externality**: pollution
- **To discourage the consumption of import and therefore protecting domestic firms**: tariffs on rival products.
- **To influence economic activity**: can change aggregate demand.

### Classification
#### Indirect taxes
> Taxes on income and wealth
- **income** **tax**: employment&investment
- **corporation** **tax**: tax on profits and firms
- **capital gains tax**: tax on profits of assets
- **Inheritance tax**: tax on wealth when a person passed away
#### Indirect taxes
> Taxes on expenditure
- **Sales tax**: GST&VAT on products sold
- **Excise duties**: tax on certain domestically produced goods - alcohol, tobacco, petrol
- **Customs duties**: taxes on imports - tariffs;
- **Licenses** for the use of televisions, cars
