## Low Inflation
> [!def] inflation
> rate of change of average prices

key measure: **CPI**
inflation affects *value of money*
high inflation hurts *spending power*
## Economic Growth
> [!def] economic growth
> rate of change of a countries' output

key measure: **GDP(Gross Domestic Product)**
> Actual Economic Growth: increase in **output**
> Potential Economic Growth: increase in **productive capacity**

> Aggregate Demand: total demand for a country's products at a given price level
> Aggregate Supply: total amount of supply at a given price level

$AD = C+I+G+(X-M)$
Consumption + Investment + Government spending + (Exports - Imports)
#### Benefits
- job creation
- rising income

## Low Unemployment
> [!def] Unemployment
> People are willing and able to work, actively seeking employment, but unable to find work.

> Economically active: willing and able to work
> Economically inactive: don't want to work

Unemployment is **waste of resources**
Unemployment rate = Unemployment / Labour force
## Payment's stability
> economic activities with other countries

If Export > Imports = **Surplus**
If Imports > Exports = **Deficit**
a **surplus or equilibrium** is the target
## Redistribution of income
> Reduce inequality by tax and social welfare

> Income inequality: focus on peoples' ability to **earn**
> Wealth Inequality: focus on stock of **financial assets**

An unequal distribution of income and wealth may result in an *unsatisfactory allocation of resources*

## Conflicts
> changes in each macroeconomic variable will impact on other variables

| **Trade-off**                                              | **Explanation**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Economic** **Growth** and **Inflation**                  | - Increasing **economic growth** causes the economy to move closer to **full employment**<br>    <br>- Prices for remaining resources are bid up leading to inflation which may outpace the **target inflation rate of 2%**                                                                                                    |
| **Economic Growth** and **Environmental Sustainability**   | - Economic growth often increases **pollution,** **negative externalities** and the **depletion of non-renewable resources**<br>    <br>- The higher the growth, the faster the depletion                                                                                                                                      |
| **Economic Growth** and **Inequality**                     | - During periods of high economic growth, the **profits the owners** of the factors of production receive are **disproportionate** to any increase in **workers' wages** leading to greater inequality                                                                                                                         |
| **Economic Growth** and **Balancing the Current Account**  | - Economic growth usually leads to **higher incomes** which leads to an increase in **imports** by households thereby **worsening** the **current account balance**                                                                                                                                                            |
| **Low Unemployment** and **Low Inflation**                 | - The closer an economy moves to **full employment** the less workers will be available for hire and **wage inflation** will help increase **overall inflation**                                                                                                                                                               |
| **Low Unemployment** and **Balancing the Current Account** | - When unemployment is low, incomes are higher and **imports increase** which worsens the current account balance<br>    <br>- Additionally, with low unemployment **wages tend to increase** which increases costs of production for firms and if they increase their prices, then the level of **exports is likely to fall** |
