> [!def]
> key characteristics of a popular market or industry

> It is a description of the characteristics that buyers encounter and sellers face in a given industry

- Number of firms
- Power and Quantity of buyers
- Degree of Product Differentiation
- Profitability
- Nature of Costs
- Consumer loyalty
- Market Share of Firms
## Break Even Point
> point when *total revenue* = *total costs*

$$\text{Break-even level of output} = \frac{\text{total fixed cost}}{\text{price/unit} - \text{variable cost/unit}}$$
## Objective of firms
- Survival - *small firms*
- Social welfare - *social enterprise*
- Growth
- Profit and profit maximisation

## Barriers to Entry
> [!def]
> obstacles that prevent firms from entering the market

- Legal barriers - e.g. patents
- Economies of scale
- Brand loyalty
- Control of technologies and resources
- Vertical integration
- Capital and expertise

## Compete
### Ways to compete
- Price
- Advertisement
- Quality
- Customer service
- Specialist products
### Compete For
- Customer base
- Sale
- Market Share
- Product superiority
- Enhance their image and image of products

### Type of Competition
#### Price
> competing with rivals suppliers on price
#### Non-price
> competing with rivals suppliers on other product features
- After sales care
### Perfect competition
#### Assumptions
- Large number of firm
- price takers
- homogenous products
- no barriers to entry and exit
- perfect factor mobility
- no advertisement needed
- no customer mobility

### Imperfect competition
#### Monopoly
> [!def] 
> a single firm or group of firms acting together with **sufficient market power** to **restrict competition** and **set** the market price

a **pure monopoly** controls the total supply of a product to a market
it may **restrict supply** to force up the market price ad earn **abnormal profits**

> firms gain *monopoly power* as a result of **barriers to entry**
#### Oligopoly
> few firms
#### Monopolistic
> firms differentiate their products
