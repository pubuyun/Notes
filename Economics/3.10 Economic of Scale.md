> [!def]
> cost advantages that enterprises obtain due to their scale of operation, which reduce *average cost* of production

![image.png|500](https://pu6uyun-image.oss-cn-hongkong.aliyuncs.com/20250417162734.png)

### Internal
> Cost savings that occur **within a firm** when it increases its own production. 

The firm becomes more efficient, reducing its average cost per unit.
### External
> Cost savings that occur when the **whole industry grows**, even if individual firms don't expand. 

More firms mean better suppliers, more skilled labour, and shared services that reduce costs for everyone.
## Diseconomies of scale
> [!def]
> cost disadvantages that economic actors accrue due to an **increase** in *organizational size* or in output

### Internal
> When a **single firm** becomes too large or inefficient, its **average cost increases**.

### External
> When the **entire industry** grows too large, it creates pressure on shared resources.
