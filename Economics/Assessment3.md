**First, we will build to boost supply.** Using **revenue from a progressive tax** on *extreme wealth*, the state will build the infrastructure the private sector neglects—like *clean energy*, *modern ports*, and affordable housing. This directly increases the supply of essentials, which will bring down prices for everyone.

**Second, we will take control of key sectors.**  Bringing energy and rail under *public ownership* ensures they **work to increase capacity** for the *public* *good*, not private profit. This guarantees stable prices and steady investment in our country's basic needs.

**Finally, we will train our people.** We will pass a law requiring large companies to fund *high-quality apprenticeship* and *worker training programs*. This is a direct investment in our *most crucial supply-side asset*: our people's skills, raising *productivity and wages* together.

This is how we increase aggregate supply—not for **corporate** **balance** **sheets**, but for the prosperity and security of all citizens."

### **Supply-Side Policy Framework**

Objective: To combat inflation and inequality by directly increasing aggregate supply for public benefit.

Measures:
1. Infrastructure Investment
    - Action: Fund to build critical infrastructure.
    - Goal: Increase the supply of essential goods and services to lower prices.
2. Nationalization
    - Action: Bring key sectors under public control.
    - Goal: Ensure these sectors prioritize ixncreasing capacity and the public good over private profit, guaranteeing stable prices and long-term investment.
3. Workforce Training
    - Action: Requirement for large companies to fund  apprenticeship and worker training programs.
    - Goal: Directly invest in human capital to raise productivity and wages.
