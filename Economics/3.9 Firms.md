## Sectors
*interdependence*: Three sectors of industry is dependent upon one another.
### Primary Sector
> [!def]
> Sectors that acquire *raw mateals*

> Produce goods using natural resources
### Secondary Sector
> [!def]
> Sectors that *assemble* and *manufacture*

*Manufacture*: produce goods from raw materials
### Tertiary Sector
> [!def]
> Sectors that produce a *service*
## Private Sector
> [!def]
> *privately owned* and operated by individuals, groups, or entities like sole proprietors, partnerships, or LLCs, *aimed for profit*

## Public Sector
> [!def]
> *government-owned* and managed to *serve public interests*

|                | Ownership  | Motive           | Source of capital | Employment Benefits | Ease of Business                 | Areas                                   |
| -------------- | ---------- | ---------------- | ----------------- | ------------------- | -------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Private Sector | individual | profit           | revenue, loan     | high income         | Easy to start, fewer regulations | Retail, tech, manufacturing, services   |
| Public Sector  | government | public interests | tax               | stable              | Slower, more bureaucracy         | Healthcare, education, public transport |

## Liability
> [!def] 
> being legally responsible for any debts or financial commitments of a business
### Unincorporated
> *Unlimited Liability*

No limit on the amount that owners have to pay
Owners pay through their personal possessions

#### Sole trader
> an individual owning the business on his/her own

Most are small businesses. e.g. hairdressers, gardeners, plumbers and electricians
**Can employee people**, but don't share ownership.
##### Advantages
- owners make all decisions, and keeps all profits
- small, need little capital
- easy to set up
- respond quickly to changes
- Individualized services can be provided
- Private accounts
##### Disadvantages
- difficult to take holidays
- hard to raise capitals (private account)
- illness can cause problems
- unlimited liability

#### Partnership
> formed where a business is started and owned by more than one person

e.g. doctors, solicitors, vets
A legal document, called a *partnership agreement* is recommended
##### Disadvantage
- Unlimited liability
- Possible disagreement
- Complications of partners leaving or joining
- legal costs of drawing up a deed
### Incorporated
> *Limited Liability*

Limit on the amount that owners have to pay
Owners do **not** have to pay through their personal possessions
#### Limited companies
> a company if formed when a business is set up to have a *separate legal identity* from its owners.
> owners -> **shareholders**
##### Private Limited Companies
> shares can only be sold **privately**
##### Public Limited Companies
> shares can be sold on the **stock market**

#### Disadvantages
- Decisions takes longer
- Expensive to set up
- Shares can only be sold privately
- Have to share profits i.e. pay dividends
- Accounts are not private

## Growth
### Internal (Organic)
> [!def]
> expansion from within a business
- opening new outlets
- expansion oversea
- new products
- technology
#### Benefit
> Less risk
> financed through internal funds
> builds on a business' strengths
> grow at a sensible rate
#### Disadvantages
> dependent on overall market
> hard to build market share
> slow growth
### External (Inorganic)
> [!def]
> achieved by takeover or merger, integrate two businesses together
- takeover - **one business takeover another**
- merger - **two combine together to become one new joint organization**
#### Benefits
> reduced competition, increased market share
> companies may allow a transfer of knowledge, skills or technology
#### Disadvantages
> grows too large, which means it may be inefficient in management, communication or worker motivation
> resentment and clashes or culture
> possible redundancies
### Measurement
> *market share* (percentage of one business's products in the whole market)

## Integration
### Backward Vertical
> Integrate with businesses in the **earlier stage** in *supply chain*
#### Benefits
- assured supplier
- preventing competitors
- Higher profit margin/added value
- cost controlled
### Horizonal
> Integrate with businesses in the **same stage** in *supply chain*
#### Benefits
- more market share
- opportunity of *economics of scale*
- less competitor
### Forward Vertical
> Integrate with businesses in the **later stage** in *supply chain*
#### Benefits
- assured outlet
- preventing competitors' goods selling 
- Higher profit margin/added value
- Information of customer preferences
### Conglomerate
> Integrate with businesses in a **unrelated industry**
#### Benefits
- spread risk
- transfer of ideas and operations

### Disadvantages
> profit **absorbed** by other businesses
