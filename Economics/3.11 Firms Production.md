## Production
> [!def]
> process of *taking resources* and change them into *goods* and *services*

Goods and services are produced to satisfy consumer's need and wants
The **production** of goods and services is organised by *entrepreneurs* in firms
A *firm* combines *land, labour and capital* (**inputs**) to make *goods and services* (**outputs**)
### Factor substitution
> [!def]
> Substitution of capital or labour in production processes as productivity of capital equipment increase.

### Productivity
> [!def] 
> use a fixed amount of **input**, how much **output** can be produced
#### Improving
- training employees
- rewarding increased productivity
- increasing job satisfaction
- division of labour
- factor substitution
### Value added
> [!def]
> difference of price of a finished product and the cost involoved in making it

$$\text{Value added} = \text{profit} + \text{wages}$$

## Labour Intensive
### Productivity
$$\text{avrage product of labour} = \frac{\text{total output/period}}{\text{number of labour}}$$
$$\text{avrage revenue of labour} = \frac{\text{total revenue/period}}{\text{number of labour}}$$
### Advantages
- **Availability and cost**: high availability, low cost at initial
- **flexible** - high occupational mobility
- **Customized**
- **Personal Service**
### Disadvantages
- **Cost**: long run cost is high
- **Low supply**: Hard to produce large amount of goods.
- **Low efficiency**
- **Labour relations**: strike
## Capital Intensive
### Advantages
- **Lack of labour shortage**
- **Uniformity**
- **Greater output**
- **Technical economics of scale**
### Disadvantages
- **Cost**: high initial cost
- **Inflexible**
- **Not for small scale**
- **Initiative**: No new ideas.
