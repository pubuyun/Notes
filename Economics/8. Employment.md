> [!def]
> the number of people in work

> People are employed if they have worked at least one hour in the last week being measured

> [!def] unemployment
> People are willing and able to work, actively seeking employment, but unable to find work.

***Level*** of *unemployment*: number of people who are unemployed
***Labour* *force***: who are **economically** **active** i.e. willing and able to work
**Cyclical unemployment**: joblessness caused by economic downturns or recessions
**Full Employment**: all **FOP** are fully utilised
## Change in Pattern
> Increase in tertiary sector and formal economy as an economy develops
> Increase in woman participation rate as greater access to education
> Decrease in the public sector as economy moves towards a market economy

## Imperfections
> powerful trade unions may restrict supply of labour to boost wage
> payment of unemployment benefits reduce the supply of labour and increase voluntary unemployment
> high non-wage costs, including employer's contributions to fund publicly provided unemployment and welfare benefits, may reduce demand of labour
> minimum wage  laws reduce the demand for less skilled labour
> labour immobility can prevent unemployed people from moving to new jobs

## Types
### Frictional
> workers move between jobs
- workers do not have perfect and immediate information about job opportunities
- typical short-term
-  **always** likely to be some frictional unemployment that persists in an economy
### Structural
> long term shifts in the structure of the economy impact upon the job market
> skill and demand in the market does not match
- tends to increase in line with the pace of globalisation
- workers need to re-train and gain new skills
### Cyclical
> heavily linked to the economic cycle, occurs when recession
- indicated that demand is low(*demand-deficient* unemployment)
- recession -> demand fallen -> no need to employ as many workers

## Consequences

- lost output. point move inward PPC, waste of economic resources
- Increased government spending on unemployment benefit
- Lost tax revenue
- Aggregate demand falls
- reduced productivity
- reduced profitability
- reduced incentive to invest
- reduced morale and productivity of remaining workforce
### Micro
- Lower living standards
- social costs
- financial costs
- de-skilling
- reduced chances of finding work
