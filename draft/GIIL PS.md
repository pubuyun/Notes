**Why are you interested in this program?**
I'm particularly interested in the Spes Nexus Program because it focuses on applying advanced technologies like AI to solve real social problems. Having built up my skills in artificial intelligence, I'm eager to move beyond theory and use what I've learned to tackle meaningful challenges—especially those that can benefit society and the environment.

What also appeals to me is the program's interdisciplinary approach. I see it as a great environment not only to strengthen my technical background but also to develop soft skills like public speaking and teamwork. By working on real cases from Stanford and Tencent SSV, and collaborating with students from different backgrounds, I expect to gain practical experience that will help me grow both personally and professionally.

In the long run, I aim to become an innovator who can bridge technology and social impact. I believe this program offers the right perspective and hands-on opportunities to help me move in that direction.

Just before our robotics competition in Hainan, we faced a serious problem. Airport security found our robot's batteries didn't meet their rules. So they took them away. Later, when we opened the robot case, we saw more damage. Several 3D-printed parts were broken during the flight.

I quickly divided the work among our team members. I started contacting other teams for help. Our hardware expert checked the damaged parts and got the files ready for printing. Another teammate went to find new batteries in local stores.

One team answered our request. They let us use their 3D printer at the competition venue. While the new parts were being printed, I changed our control software. The software needed to work with the new parts. We fixed everything before the competition started.

This difficult situation helped our team learn to work together better. It also helped us make friends with another team. We still talk with them now. We are planning to work together on future community activities.

My perspective is shaped by two key pursuits: a deep curiosity for technology and a commitment to community engagement. This combination provides me with a distinct and practical outlook.

My approach to technology centers on understanding fundamental principles. While many are content using tools, I focus on their underlying mechanics. For instance, after using Stable Diffusion for image generation, I studied its latent diffusion models to comprehend how images form from noise. Similarly, my interest in large language models led me to examine the attention mechanism through visual mapping. This foundational knowledge enables me to not only use technology but also adapt and build upon it effectively.

My community work has equally influenced my thinking. Teaching in under-resourced rural schools and distributing academic materials have demonstrated that a solution's value lies in its human impact rather than its technical sophistication. These experiences drive me to consistently consider the end-user, ensuring that what I create is both powerful and genuinely accessible to those it aims to serve.
