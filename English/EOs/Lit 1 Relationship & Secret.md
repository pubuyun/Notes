## **1. Extract Point — Early Dynamics: Power + Admiration**

**Point:** Ruth asserts dominance while Kathy admires her, creating an unequal but formative early bond.  
**Evidence:** Ruth “looked me up and down”; Kathy was “absolutely delighted”.  
**Analysis:** Shows Ruth’s confidence and Kathy’s desire to be chosen; hierarchical friendship dynamic.  
**Link:** Establishes a pattern where Kathy follows and Ruth controls.

---

## **2. Cross-Reference Part One — Friendship Built on Imitation + Confusion**

**Point:** Ruth often leads through inventions or fantasies; Kathy tries to fit in and understand.  
**Evidence:** I have no horse
**Analysis:** Kathy’s narration shows her looking back with mild criticism but continued attachment; both girls are trying to make sense of Hailsham.  
**Effect:** Friendship shaped by loyalty mixed with insecurity.

---

## **3. Cross-Reference Part Two — Growing Tensions + Betrayal**

**Point:** At the Cottages, their relationship becomes strained as Ruth becomes manipulative and competitive.  
**Evidence:** Ruth belittling Kathy about the cassette tape; pretending to be more “mature” with the veterans.  
**Analysis:** Narration reveals Kathy’s hurt but emotional restraint; structure shows friendship becoming more fragile.  
**Effect / Theme:** Explores loyalty, jealousy, and the painful shift from childhood roles.

---

## **4. Cross-Reference Part Three — Maturity, Forgiveness, and Shared Fate**

**Point:** Their friendship softens as adulthood and donations approach; facing death brings honesty.  
**Evidence:** Ruth’s apology during the boat trip; asking Kathy and Tommy to seek a deferral. 
**Analysis:** Symbolism of the abandoned boat = end of illusions; Kathy finally understands Ruth’s fears and regrets.  
**Effect:** Ishiguro uses memory to show how time reshapes friendships; dignity and forgiveness emerge at the end.
