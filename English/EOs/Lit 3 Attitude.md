In _Never Let Me Go_, Ishiguro uses Madame and Miss Emily to reveal society’s deeply conflicted attitudes towards the clones — pity, revulsion, justification, and moral self-deception. Starting with this extract, their behaviour exposes how adults rationalise cruelty while believing themselves humane.

1. uneasy moral guilt that adults feel towards the clones.
	1. Madame whispers, _“Poor creatures. What did we do to you?”_ and Kathy sees _“tears_
		1. The phrase “poor creatures” shows both compassion and dehumanisation
		2. clones are fundamentally “other.”
	2. setting: the “darkened half of the room” and the “mechanical”

		2. . Kathy’s _naïve narration_ intensifies this
	3. **This tension positions Madame as someone who sees clones as victims but still not as equals, shaping the reader’s understanding of society’s uncomfortable moral hypocrisy.**
