## Pollination
> [!def]
> The transfer of pollen grains from anther to stigma
### Self-pollination
> The transfer of pollen grains from anther of a flower to the stigma of the same flower or a different flower on the **same plant**
### Cross-pollination
> The transfer of pollen grains from the anther of a flower to the stigma of a flower on a **different plant** of the same specie

## Fertilization
> when a pollen nucleus fuses with a nucleus in an ovule

> the **fusion** of the **nuclei** of a male gamete and a female gamete to form a zygote
	
## Germination
> The development of a plant from a seed after a period of **dormancy**

***dormancy***: a period of time where growth and development is down
**Reasons**:
- To wait for optimum environment for germination
- To avoid competition: if all seeds germinate at the same time

### Sepals
> Protect the flower bud before the flower blooms and support the petals after blooming
### Petals
> Large brightly colored to attract insects
### Nectary
produce nectar (a sugary substance to attract insects)
### Stamen
> male part of the flower
#### Anther
> produce pollen (male gamete)
#### Filament
> support
### Carpel
> female part of the flower
#### Stigma
> Sticky structure that pollen attached to
#### Style
> Support stigma, connects the stigma to the ovary
#### Ovary
> Produce female gametes (with in the ovules)
> Later develop into fruit
#### Ovule
> contains the female gamete,
> Later develop into seed
### Stem
> Support flower
### Receptacle
> vegetative tissues near the end of reproductive stems that are situated below or encase the reproductive organs
