## Asexual Reproduction
> [!def] asexual reproduction
> a process resulting in the production of **genetically identical** offspring from **one** parent
### Examples
- Bacteria
- many plants
- some animals (Hydra, starfish)
### Advantages
- Only one parent is needed
- Process is quick
- Sometimes no variation is good (if well adapted)
Allows a single organism to quickly colonize a new area.
### Disadvantages
-  All the offspring are clones, 
a new disease can wipe out the whole species
less chance of adapting to new conditions
## Sexual Reproduction
> [!def] sexual reproduction
> a process involving the **fusion** of the nuclei of two *haploid gametes* (sex cells) to form a *diploid zygote* and the production of offspring that are **genetically different** from each other

> [!def] Ploidy
> the number of complete sets of [chromosomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromosome "Chromosome") in a [cell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_\(biology\) "Cell (biology)")

> *haploid* (one set of complete chromosome)
> *diploid* (two sets of complete chromosome)
> nuclei of **gametes** are *haploid*, nucleus of **zygote** is *diploid*

> A **gamete** is a [haploid cell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ploidy#Haploid_and_monoploid "Ploidy") that fuses with another haploid cell during [fertilization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fertilization "Fertilization") in [organisms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organism "Organism") that [reproduce sexually](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sexual_reproduction "Sexual reproduction")

### Examples
- Human
- most other animals
- Flowing Plants
### Advantages
- Loads of genetic variation within the population
- Population is less likely to get wiped out by a single event
- Allows for evolution to take place
### Disadvantages
- Takes a lot more time and energy (find potential mate, need to impress their mate: some feature doesn't help the organism to survive but to impress their mate)
