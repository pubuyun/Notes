## Anaerobic Respiration
> also known as *fermentation*

**example**: *Lactobacillus*, lives in intestine
### [Lactic acid fermentation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lactic_acid_fermentation)
$$C_6H_{12}O_{6}+2ADP+2P_{i}\to2CH_{3}CH(OH)COOH + 2ATP+2H_{2}O$$
or $$\text{glucose}\to \text{lactic acid}+ATP$$
> by *muscle cells*
> **decrease** the pH in blood
#### Lactic Acid
> Builds up in muscle and blood
> *must* be broken down by liver
- aerobic respiration of lactic acid:
$$\text{lactic acid+oxygen}\to \text{carbon dioxide+water+ATP}$$
#### Oxygen debt
##### Why
- When muscles are anaerobically respiring, lactic acid is made.
- They diffuse out of cells into the capillaries and eventually get transported
to liver.
- In order to break down lactic acid, which is toxic, oxygen is needed.
- The oxygen needed to break down lactic acid is the *oxygen debt*
##### How
-  continuation of fast heart rate to transport lactic acid in the blood from the muscles to the liver 
- continuation of deeper and faster breathing to supply oxygen for aerobic respiration of lactic acid 
- aerobic respiration of lactic acid in the live
