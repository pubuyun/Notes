## Menstrual Cycle
> [!def] 
> Changes in the ovaries and in the lining of the uterus over an average of 28 days

prepares a female's body for the **fertilisation** of an egg cell so that it can be supported during pregnancy

## Stages
#### *Menses* Day 1-4
> Uterus starts to **shed** its lining, occurs at the start of the cycle if no fertilisation has occurred, blood passes out.
- *Endometrium* is sloughed away (menstruation)
- ***FSH*** No longer inhibited, can start menstrual cycle again
#### **Follicular** **Phase** Day 5-14
> The **lining** starts to thicken, preparing for pregnancy. Inside the ovary a *follicle* containing an *ovum* develops

- ***FSH*** by *pituitary* *gland* 
	- **stimulates** growth of ***several** follicles*
	- **stimulates** release of ***Estrogen***
- ***Estrogen*** by ***Dominant*** *follicle* 
	- **stimulates** development of *uterus lining*
	- **stimulates** **LH** at high level
	- **inhibits** growth of other *follicles* 
	- **inhibits** ***FSH***
#### *Ovulation* Day 14-17
> Releasing the egg, the egg travels down the oviduct towards the uterus
> Eggs travel down oviduct (last for 3 days), the *follicle* turns into *corpus* *luteum* (stop uterus lining growing)
- surge in ***LH***  by *pituitary gland*
	- **stimulates** ovulation (egg release)
	- **stimulates** development of *yellow body*
	- **stimulates *Progesterone***

#### **Luteal Phase** Day 17-28
> The lining is maintained ready, becoming vascular, to accept a embryo
- ***Progesterone***  by *Yellow body*  
	- **stimulate** keeping *uterus lining* thick
	-  **inhibit** ***FSH*** and ***LH***
- ***Estrogen*** by *Yellow body*  
	- **stimulate** *uterus lining* development
	- **inhibit** ***FSH***
- Over time,
	- *Yellow body* degrades
	- ***Progesterone*** levels drop
	- *uterus lining* cannot be maintained
	- **Menstruation**
-  If **fertilisation** of egg occurs, ***Progesterone*** and ***Estrogen*** levels maintain
### Hormones
**FSH**(Follicle Stimulating Hormone) by *Pituitary* *gland*
**LH**(Luteinising Hormone) by *pituitary gland*
**Oestrogen** produced by the *ovaries*
**Testosterone** produced by *testes*
## Puberty
> **Puberty** is the time when lots of **changes** happen in your body as you develop into an adult

- become **sexually mature**, allowing reproduction
- develop **secondary sexual characteristics**
### Secondary Sexual Characteristics
#### Both
- growth spurt
- sweat more
- spots appear(acne)
- voice deepens
#### Male
- facial and body hair
- muscles develop
- voice breaks
#### Female
- breasts develop
- body hair grows
- hips get wider
