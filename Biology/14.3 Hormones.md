## Hormones
> a chemical substance, produced by the *gland* and carried by the *blood*, which alters the activity of one or more specific organs
## Pituitary gland
> affect *other* glands causing more hormones to be released

Located in the **brain** underneath the *hypothalamus*

**FSH** - follicle stimulating hormone
**LH** - luteinising hormone - stimulates ovulation(egg release)
**Growth Hormones**
**ADH** - Anti-diuretic hormone
**TSH** - Thyroid-stimulating hormone

## Adrenal Glands
> Release *adrenaline*

Located on top of the *kidneys*
Responsible for *fight or flight* mechanism with danger
- heart rate
- breathing rate
- pupil diameter
## Pancreas
> Contains cells that release *insulin* and *glucagon*

Located behind the *stomach*
Helps control blood glucose concentration
## Testes
> Release the sex hormone *testosterone*
## Ovaries 
> release *oestrogen and progesterone*

Responsible for secondary sexual characteristics: *puberty*
## Comparison

|              | Nervous                              | Endocrine                             |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------- |
| Parts        | Brain, spinal cord, nerves           | glands                                |
| Type         | Electrical impulse                   | Chemical hormone                      |
| Transmission | Nerves                               | bloodstream                           |
| Effector     | Muscles or glands                    | target cells in specific tissues      |
| Speed        | Very fast                            | Slower                                |
| Duration     | Short until electrical impulses stop | Longer - until hormone is broken down |
