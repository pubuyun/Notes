## Eye
![image.png](https://pu6uyun-image.oss-cn-hongkong.aliyuncs.com/20250515125949.png)

*Cornea*: refracts light
*Iris*: controls how much light enters the pupil
*Lens*:  focuses light on to the retina
*Retina*: contains light receptors, some sensitive to light of different colours
*Optic Nerve*: carries nerve impulses from the eye to the brain
*Blind Spot*: the part of the retina where the optic nerve leaves, and where there are no receptor cells
*Fovea*: The part of the retina where cone cells are tightly packed (clearer image), where light is focused then you look directly at an object)
*Rods*: sensory to dim light, no colour
*Cones*: Distinguish between different colours, only function under bright light, RGB.

## Iris Reflex (Pupil Reflex)
> An automatic response to a change in light intensity

*Receptor*: retina
*Effector*: Radial muscle, Circular muscle
### Circular Muscle
> smaller radius when **contract**
### Radial muscle
> smaller radius when **relax**

![[Pasted image 20250522130916.png|300]]

|                 | Pupil Dilation | Pupil Constriction |
| --------------- | -------------- | ------------------ |
| Light Intensity | ↓              | ↑                  |
| Radial Muscle   | contract       | relax              |
| Circular Muscle | relax          | contract           |
| Pupil Size      | ↑              | ↓                  |
|                 |                |                    |
### Antagonistic Muscles
> muscles that opposes the action of another

#### Example
- Circular and radial muscles
- Biceps and Triceps
## Accommodation
> the way the lens brings about fine focusing

### Ciliary Muscle
> thicker when **contract**
### Suspensory Ligaments
> thicker when **loose**
