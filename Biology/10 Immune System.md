## Immune System
### Passive immunity
> [!def] 
> when someone has been given ready-made antibodies

> Breastfeeding, injection
### Active immunity
> [!def]
> when the immune system makes their own antibodies to a specific pathogen

> Catching a disease, getting a virus vaccination
## Antibodies
> Proteins
> *antigen* is usually on the outside a pathogen, specifically shaped. Body can recognize it as *foreign* and start producing antibodies
### Specificity
>  Each kind of lymphocyte makes a specific kind of antibody
>  complementary shaped to a specific *antigen*
### Memory cells
- Lymphocyte clones itself when a pathogen is encountered
- Some of the new cells **remain in the blood and lymph for a very long time**, instead of making antibodies - *memory cells*
- Memory cells ensure **faster response** when the same pathogen appears the second time

## Vaccination
- Weakened or dead pathogen
- The antigen of pathogen
- The genetic code of antigen
## Pathogen
> [!def] 
> disease-causing organism

> a microorganism that causes disease in a host(an organism in which pathogen lives and reproduces)
### Types
- *Virus*: influenza流感, common cold普通感冒, poliomyelitis脊髓灰质炎, measles麻疹, AIDS艾滋病
- *Bacteria*: cholera霍乱, syphilis梅毒, whooping cough百日咳, tuberculosis肺结核, tetanus破伤风
- *Protoctista*: malaria疟疾, amoebic dysentery阿米巴痢疾
- *Fungi*: athlete's foot足癣, ringworm癣
#### Cholera
- caused by *Vibrio cholerae*
- Cholera releases toxin 
- small intestine secrete chloride ions
- lower water potential
- Osmosis happens
- Diarrhea/dehydration
### Direct Transmission
> Host directly contacting another uninfected organism
> **body fluid**: blood, saliva mucus, sexual contact
> Faeces
> Skin touch
> Breastfeeding, childbirth

### Indirect Transmission
> *Most pathogens are transmitted indirectly*
> Breathing in **droplets** containing pathogens
> **Touching a surface** that has pathogens
> Eating **food** or drinking **water** that has pathogens
> **Swimming** in water that has pathogens
> **Contacting animal** that are carrying pathogens

## Body Defences
**Skin**: First barrier. If the skin is broken, a blood clot would form
**Stomach**: HCl Acid that kills bacteria in food
**Hairs in the nose**: Filter air particles from the air that could have pathogens
**Sense of smell and taste**: Bad smell or taste make us sick
**Mucus in airway**: Traps bacteria and mucus is swallowed instead of going in the lungs,
**Immune System**: Engulf pathogens or make antibodies
