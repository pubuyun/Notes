## Sustainable Resources
> [!def]
>  one which is produced **as rapidly as it is removed** from the environment so that it does not run out
### Reasons
- maintaining or increasing **biodiversity** 
- reducing **extinction**
- protecting vulnerable **ecosystems**
- maintaining **ecosystem functions**
	- nutrient cycling 
	- resource provision
		- food, drugs, fuel, genes
### Forests
- **Selective Felling**
- Education
- Protected areas
- Quotas and replanting
### Fish
- Education
- Closed seasons
- Protected areas
- Controlled net types and mesh size
- Quotas and monitoring
## Endangered Species
> Extinct or has a risk to extinct
### Why
- **Climate change**
	- Sudden or long-term change in environment
- **Habitat destruction**
	- housing, agriculture, mining
- **Hunting**
	- hunting for food, sport, or illegal trade (e.g. skin)
- **Overharvesting**
	- Taking wild plants faster than they can reproduce
- Pollution and **Introduced Species**
	- Non-native species may outcompete local species
### How
- Monitoring and protecting species and habitats
- Education
- Captive breeding programmes
	- Endangered animals bred in zoos
- seed banks
	- Seed from rare or endangered plants
	- Allow Replanting
### Risk
- Low Genetic Variation
- Inbreeding
- Lower disease resistance
