> [!def] homeostasis
> The maintenance of a constant internal environment, by *Negative feedback* with reference to a **set point**.

> **Homeo** *Stasis*
> **The same** *Stable*

> [!def] Negative Feedback
> The process of **detecting** a change **away** from the set point, and bringing it **back** toward the set point
## Temperature
> maintain at **37℃**, optimum temperature for enzymes and chemical reactions they catalyse

monitored by hypothalamus/thermoregulary centre in the brain
### Importance
> Enzymes do not denature
> No damage to (named) proteins
> Maintain a constant rate of reaction/respiration
> Avoid heatstroke/hyperthermia/overheating/too cold/hypothermia/freezing
### Too low: 
- metabolic reactions are slowed
- *Shivering* increases metabolism rate in muscles to release heat
- Hair stands up
- Increased adrenaline secretion
- Vasoconstriction
- Insulation
- Animals curl up or huddle together(behaviour change)
### Too high:
- enzymes are denatured, reactions stop
- *Sweating*
- Hair lies flat
- Vasodilation
- Animals lie spread out(behaviour change)

## Blood Glucose Concentration
### Low
- Not enough glucose for **respiration** to release energy
- Can lead to tiredness, weakness, dizziness
### High
- Can cause water to move out of cells by osmosis
- Can lead to blood vessels that carry blood to vital organs being damaged, which increase the risk of heart disease

#### Hormones
> **Insulin** released to decrease blood glucose concentration
> **Glucagon** released to increase blood glucose concentration

#### Insulin
- stimulates liver to synthesis Glycogen from glucose
- stimulates cells to do respiration
#### Glucagon
- stimulates liver to decompose glycogen to release glucose
- reduce cell respiration
