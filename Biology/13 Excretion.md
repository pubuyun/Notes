> [!def]
> Removal of waste products of __Metabolism__ and substances in excess
## Why
> **Excess water and salt minerals**: maintain blood volume and concentration so cells don't swell/shrink
> **CO2**: dissolved CO2 lowers blood pH and is toxic to cells
> **Urea**: contains ammonia which is highly toxic

## Process
### Ultrafilteration
![image|250](https://pu6uyun-image.oss-cn-hongkong.aliyuncs.com/20250423104604.png)
*Glomerulus* (肾小球): **capillaries** form a bundle
Bowman's capsule (肾小囊)

> The small parts of the blood(**amino acids, glucose, water, ions, urea**) are **forced out** into the Bowman's capsule
> Blood cells and large proteins remains in the blood.

### Reabsorption
> fluid moves along the nephron(renal tubule)
> It's now called *glomerular filtrate*(原尿)
> *capillaries* continues around the nephron, which **absorbs** useful products(**100% glucose**, **some** water, amino acids, ions ) by **diffusion** and **active transport**

## Urea
> [!def] 
> a waste product formed from **excess amino acids**

Urea in blood is *toxic*
### Where made
> Absorbed amino acids flow from villi, to hepatic portal vein, to the liver
> Urea formed by the **liver** through ***deamination***(脱氨)

> [!def] deamination
> The removal of the nitrogen-containing part of amino acid
