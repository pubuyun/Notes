## Nose + (Mouth)
> Where air enters
### Hair
> Filter out particles and pathogens
### Goblet Cells
> Make a liquid that contains water and mucus
> Water evaporates to **moisten** the air
> Mucus **traps dust and pathogen**
### Ciliated Cell
> Have *cilia* that are always moving
> They **sweep mucus** to the back of the throat (pharynx) so it doesn't go into lungs
## Pharynx 咽
> Also known as the *throat*
- Both digestive and respiratory system share the pharynx 
## Larynx 喉
> Also known as the *voice box*
- Contains vocal cords
## Trachea 气管
> Also known as *windpipe*
- Contains goblet cells and ciliated cells
- Contains rings of cartilage
### Cartilage 软骨
> To **hold airway open**, **prevent collapse**
> *incomplete ring* - flexibility, **protection**
> C - shaped

## Bronchus and Bronchiole 支气管和细支气管
- Trachea divides into two: **left** and **right** *bronchus*. Pl: *bronchi*
- It further branches out into bronchioles.
- They have *ciliated* and *goblet* cells, too.

## Alveoli 肺泡
> **tiny air sacs** at the end of each bronchiole branch s. Alveolus
> balloon-like, expands when air goes in
![|300](https://pu6uyun-image.oss-cn-hongkong.aliyuncs.com/20250326103324.png)
### Adaptation
**One cell thick walls**: short diffusion distance
**Surrounded by capillaries**: blood constantly coming from pulmonary artery, and leaving through pulmonary vein
**Large surface area**: total area of alveoli is over 100 square meters
**Good ventilation**: air coming in and out of lungs is constant, *maintain concentration gradient*
**Moist surface**: Gas must be dissolved in liquid to diffuse
# Muscle
## Diaphragm
> a thick muscle below the lungs
> Originally dome-shaped
- *Contracts* when **inhaling**
- *Relaxes* when **exhaling**
## Intercostal muscles
> two layers of muscles in between the **ribs**
> to help the expanding and relaxing of the ribcage during breathing

**external intercostal muscle**: outer
**internal intercostal muscle**: inner
### Inspiration
**External intercostal**: contracts to elevate ribs
**Diaphragm**: contracts to expand thorax (chest)
### Expiration
**External intercostal**: relaxes to return ribs
**Diaphragm**: relaxes to reduce volume of thorax


|                            | External Intercostal | Internal Intercostal | Diaphragm       | Ribcage              | Volume of throax |
| -------------------------- | -------------------- | -------------------- | --------------- | -------------------- | ---------------- |
| **Inhalation Inspiration** | Contracts            | Relaxes              | Lower Contracts | Rises, Goes outwards | Increase         |
| **Exhalation Expiration**  | Relaxes              | Contracts            | Higher Relaxes  | Lower, Goes inwards  | Decrease         |

## Exercise
> Higher **respiration rate** and **muscle contraction**
> **Brain detects** increased concentration of $CO_{2}$ in the blood by a **receptor**
> it send impulses(冲动) to muscles to increase *rate* and *depth* of breathing by **neuron**

## Damage
#### Carcinogen(致癌物)
- cause lung cancer
- damages air passages
- trigger production of excess mucus
#### Smoke particles
- trigger WBC
- prone to infection
#### Carbon Monoxide
- combines *permanently* with *haemoglobin*
- reduced oxygen transport of blood
