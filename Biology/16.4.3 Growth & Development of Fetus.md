## Pregnancy: Fetal Growth & Development
> [!def]
> **Gestation period**: the time during which an embryo develops in the uterus, approximately 9 months in humans.

### Fertilisation to Implantation
> **Zygote**: the cell formed by the fusion of a male and female gamete at fertilisation.

 - Fertilisation occurs in the **oviduct**.
 - The zygote undergoes cell division as it travels to the **uterus** (about 3 days).
 - The resulting ball of cells is called an **embryo**.
 - **Implantation**: The embryo embeds into the thickened uterus lining;  .

### Development Stages
> **Embryo**: the developing organism from fertilisation until the end of the 8th week.
> **Fetus**: the developing organism from the 9th week of pregnancy until birth.
1.  **First 12 Weeks (Major Organ Development)**:
    - Major organs and body systems form (**organogenesis**).
    - Nutrients and oxygen are obtained from the mother by **diffusion through the uterus lining**.
    - The **placenta** forms.
    - The embryo is now termed a **fetus**.
2.  **Remaining Gestation (Growth)**:
    - The primary focus is on **growth in size** and maturation of organs.

## Placenta & Umbilical Cord
> **Placenta**: an organ that develops in the uterus during pregnancy, allowing the exchange of materials between the mother's blood and the fetus's blood.
> **Umbilical Cord**: a cord containing blood vessels that connects the fetus to the placenta.
- The fetus is connected to the placenta via the **umbilical cord**.
- The mother's and fetus's blood **do not mix**; they flow in close proximity.
- **Substances exchanged by diffusion**:
    - **To the fetus**: Oxygen, glucose, amino acids, fats, water.
    - **From the fetus**: Carbon dioxide, urea (and other wastes).
- **Adaptations of the placenta**:
    - **Large surface area** for efficient exchange.
    - **Thin wall** to provide a short diffusion distance.

### Amniotic Sac
> **Amniotic Sac**: a membrane that surrounds the fetus.
> **Amniotic Fluid**: the fluid contained within the amniotic sac, produced from the mother's blood plasma.
- The fetus is surrounded by the **amniotic sac**, filled with **amniotic fluid**.
- **Function**: **Cushions the fetus**, protecting it from physical bumps and shocks.

### Barrier Function & Risks
- The placenta acts as a **barrier**, but it is **not completely effective**.
- **Toxins** (e.g., **nicotine** from smoking) and some **pathogens** (e.g., the **rubella virus**) can cross the placenta and harm the fetus.
