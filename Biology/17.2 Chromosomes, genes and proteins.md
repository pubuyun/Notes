## Chromosomes
> [!def] **Chromosomes**
> structures made of DNA that contain genetic information in the form of genes

> **Gene**: a length of DNA that codes for a protein
> **Allele**: an alternative form of a gene

> **Haploid Nucleus**: a nucleus containing a single set of chromosomes (n)
> **Diploid Nucleus**: a nucleus containing two sets of chromosomes (2n)
> **Human Diploid Cells**: contain 23 pairs of chromosomes (46 total)
## Genes
> [!def]
> **Gene Expression**: the sequence of bases in a gene determines the sequence of amino acids used to make a specific protein

> **Gene Expression Specificity**: most body cells in an organism contain the same genes, but many genes in a particular cell are not expressed because the cell only makes the specific proteins it needs
### Function
> DNA controls cell function by controlling the production of proteins, including:
- Enzymes
- Membrane carriers
- Receptors for neurotransmitters
> **Sex Inheritance in Humans**: determined by X and Y chromosomes

 - Females: **XX**
 - Males: **XY**
## Proteins
> [!def]
> **Protein Structure**: different sequences of amino acids give different shapes to protein molecules
### Protein Synthesis Process
**mRNA**: Messenger RNA, copy of a gene, only one strand
**rRNA**: ribosomial RNA, catalyse the form of connection between amino acids
**tRNA**: transfer RNA, contains anti-codon and a specific amino acid; delivers the correct amino acids to the ribosome
#### Transcription
1. The **gene** coding for the protein remains in the **nucleus**
2. **Messenger RNA (mRNA)** is a copy of a gene
3. mRNA molecules are made in the nucleus and move to the **cytoplasm**
4. The mRNA passes through **ribosomes**
#### Translation
1. The ribosome assembles **amino acids** into protein molecules
2. The specific sequence of amino acids is determined by the **sequence of bases** in the mRNA
