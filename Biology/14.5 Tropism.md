## Sensitivity
Plants respond to stimuli to:
- avoid stress
- avoid being eaten
- enhance survival
- improve chances of having offspring
## \*Tropism
>  a **tropism** is a [phenomenon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenomenon "Phenomenon") indicating the growth or turning movement of an [organism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organism "Organism"), usually a [plant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plant "Plant"), in response to an environmental [stimulus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stimulus_\(physiology\) "Stimulus (physiology)")

## Geotropism
> a response in which parts of a plant grow towards or away from gravity

**Positive**: towards earth
**Negative**: away from earth
## Phototropism
> a response in which parts of a plant grow towards or away from the direction of the light source

**Positive**: towards light
**Negative**: away from light
## Auxin
> a hormone, controls shoot growth
- made in the shoot tip
- diffuses through the plant from the shoot tip
- unequally distributed in response to light and gravity
- stimulates cell elongation(cell becomes longer)

### Positive Phototropism
> Sun overhead, auxins get **distributed evenly** on all sides
> Sun hits from a side, auxin move to the **shaded** side, **inducing** cell *elongation*
### Positive Gravitropism
> auxin diffuses down the root
> auxin collects on the lower slide
> auxin **inhibits** cell elongation in the lower side of roots
> roots bend toward gravity
#### Reasons
- reach water/ions or minerals
- anchor plants in soil
### Negative Gravitropism
> auxin diffuses away from tip down the stem/shoot/plant
> auxin collects on the lower side, stimulates cell elongation
> plant/stem bends upward
#### Reasons
- reach light 
- reach air for carbon dioxide, oxygen
- flowers exposed for pollination
